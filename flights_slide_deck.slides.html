Investigation Overview¶

In this investigation I wanted to discover the most frequentied reason that affects the delay of the flights. And to know the percentage of each delay reasons. Also I wanted to discover the variation of the delay reasons across the years (from 2004 to 2020) and the average delayed time in both year and month.

Dataset Overview¶

This dataset reports flights in the United States, including carriers, arrival and departure delays, and reasons for delays, from 2004 to 2020.

(Relation Between Average Total Delayed Arrival Time (min) And Year)¶

The line plot shows the variation of the delay reasons over the years froom 2004 to 2020. And it shows that 2016 has the most delayed time which is more than 5000 minutes. And 2020 has the least delayed time which is around 1500 minutes.

(Average Delayed Arrival Time Per Year And Month)¶

The next heat map shows the average total delayed time in both year and month. July 2016 has the highest average delayed time for arrival. And July 2005 comes in the second position. I noticed in 2020 from April to December, the average total delayed time was approximatly lower than the rest of the years and maybe that because of the high rate of flights cancellation and the lockdown time as a result of the covid-19 pandemic. Which means there was low number of flights during 2020.

(Delay Reasons Per Year)¶

The figure shows the variation of the delay reasons over the years. It shows that the least delay was because of security issue, and the most delay was because of late aircraft issue. We can notice that the most number of cancelled flights was in 2020 and we can conclude that it was because of the covid 19 pandemic and the lockdown time.

total_no_of_flights = df.arr_flights_on_time.sum() + df.carrier_count.sum() + df.weather_count.sum() + df.nas_count.sum() + df.security_count.sum() + df.late_aircraft_count.sum() + df.arr_cancelled_count.sum() + df.arr_diverted_count.sum()
